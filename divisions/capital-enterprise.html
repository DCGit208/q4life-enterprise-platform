Investment Process & Timeline

Efficient, transparent process from initial contact to funding‚Äîtypically 30-60 days for most transactions

1

Initial Screening (48 Hours)

Submit executive summary, financials (trailing 12 months), and capital request. Investment committee reviews and provides preliminary feedback within 2 business days on fit and potential terms.

  • Required: 3-year historical financials, current TTM results, capitalization table, uses/sources
  • NDA signed, materials handled confidentially by IC members only
  • Decision: Decline (45%), Request more info (35%), Proceed to term sheet (20%)
2

Management Meeting & Term Sheet (1 Week)

Virtual or in-person meeting with management team and sponsors (if applicable) to discuss business model, growth plan, competitive positioning, and capital needs. Non-binding term sheet issued within 3-5 days if proceeding.

  • 2-3 hour meeting covering: business overview, financial deep-dive, capital structure discussion
  • Term sheet outlines: amount, structure, pricing, covenants, governance, fees, timeline
  • Exclusivity period: 45-60 days to complete diligence and close (30 days for time-sensitive deals)
3

Due Diligence (3-4 Weeks)

Comprehensive financial, legal, and commercial diligence conducted by Q4 team and third-party advisors. Diligence scope and timeline scaled to transaction size and complexity.

  • Financial DD: Quality of earnings, working capital analysis, cash flow review (Big 4 for deals >$25M)
  • Legal DD: Material contracts, litigation, IP, compliance (top-tier law firms, red flag items only)
  • Commercial DD: Customer/supplier calls, market analysis, competitive assessment (optional for deals <$15M)
  • Virtual data room setup, weekly status calls, transparent communication throughout
4

Documentation & Closing (2-3 Weeks)

Credit committee approval, definitive documentation preparation, and closing logistics. Legal teams negotiate final terms, documents are executed, and funds are wired on closing date.

  • Credit committee presentation: Investment memo, diligence findings, sensitivity analysis, approval vote
  • Definitive docs: Credit agreement, security documents, legal opinions, closing certificates (150-300 pages typical)
  • Closing: All parties sign, conditions precedent satisfied, funds wired same-day, portfolio monitoring begins

Borrower Qualifications & Requirements

We invest across industries and structures‚Äîhere's what we look for in potential portfolio companies

üìä

Financial Profile

  • Revenue Scale: Minimum $5M annual revenue for most strategies; early-stage venture debt can go as low as $2M with strong growth trajectory (>100% YoY)
  • Profitability: EBITDA-positive preferred but not required; will consider high-growth companies with clear path to profitability within 12-18 months
  • Financial Reporting: Clean accounting (GAAP/accrual basis), monthly financials, annual audit or review required for investments >$15M
  • Leverage Tolerance: Total debt/EBITDA typically capped at 5.5-6.0x for senior positions, higher for subordinated/mezzanine structures

Business Characteristics

  • Market Position: Defensible competitive position, recurring revenue or repeat customers preferred, niche leadership or top-3 market position
  • Management Team: Experienced operators with skin in the game (10%+ ownership preferred), track record of execution, willingness to work collaboratively
  • Industry Focus: Sector-agnostic but prefer B2B services, software, healthcare, industrials; avoid early-stage biotech, pre-revenue hardware, highly regulated industries
  • Growth Trajectory: Historical growth >15% annually, clear growth drivers (new products, M&A, geographic expansion), proven customer acquisition model

Investment Thresholds

  • Check Size: Minimum $2M per transaction (smaller for venture debt/SAFEs with existing relationships), typical range $5M-$50M, can go up to $100M+ for club deals
  • Equity Sponsor: Not required‚Äîwe invest in sponsor-backed and non-sponsored companies; institutional backing can streamline diligence and improve terms
  • Use of Proceeds: Growth capital, acquisitions, recaps, refinancing, shareholder liquidity all acceptable; no dividend-only recaps or asset stripping
  • Geography: North America primary focus (US/Canada), will consider Western Europe and select LATAM markets for deals >$25M with local partners

Structural Requirements

  • Security: First-lien on all assets for senior debt, second-lien for sub-debt, blanket UCC filings, mortgages on real estate >$5M value
  • Covenants: Financial maintenance covenants for senior/unitranche (leverage, FCCR, min EBITDA), covenant-lite for sub-debt with equity kicker
  • Reporting: Monthly financial statements within 15 days, quarterly compliance certificates, annual audited financials, budget/forecast updates
  • Governance: Board observer rights typical for equity/structured investments, consent rights on major actions (M&A >$5M, new debt, asset sales)

  • Financial Issues: Material weaknesses in internal controls, recurring restatements, frequent CFO turnover, cash discrepancies, unpaid payroll taxes
  • Operational Red Flags: Customer churn >25% annually, negative gross margins, inventory obsolescence >15%, related-party transactions without arm's length pricing
