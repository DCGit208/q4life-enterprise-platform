Core Thesis

We are not building a car business.

We are building transaction infrastructure for automotive markets — the rails that money flows through:

  • Financing commissions

  • Data licensing

Analogies:

  • Stripe doesn't own inventory — they own payment rails

We combine all three: Assets + Labor + Transactions + Platform

Revenue Stream #2: Vehicle Financing (The Billion-Dollar Layer)

Banks don't want small retail clients (high admin cost, low margins). We become the intermediary — aggregating demand, underwriting clients, routing to financial partners.

Commission Structure

  • Financing Referral: 5-12% of loan value
  • Example: Client finances $10,000 car → We earn $500-$1,200 instantly
  • Scale: 100 financed cars/month = $50k-$120k/month
  • Risk: Zero (bank holds the loan, we earn commission)

Why This Matters: Financing commissions often EXCEED sales commissions. A $10k car at 10% financing commission = $1,000 (vs $300 sales commission at 3%). This is where billions live.

Financial Partners

  • Commercial banks (retail auto loans)
  • Microfinance institutions (informal sector)
  • Saccos/cooperatives (member financing)
  • International lenders (diaspora financing)

PART 2: Marketplace Platform Architecture

The marketplace isn't a product. It's infrastructure that connects:

  • Buyers + Sellers
  • Financial institutions + Borrowers
  • Insurance providers + Vehicle owners
  • Mechanics + Customers
  • Parts suppliers + Repair shops
  • Fleet owners + Corporate clients

Platform Architecture: 7 Ownership Layers

You Own ALL 7 Layers:

  1. ✅ Vehicle Transactions — Marketplace commissions (3-7%)
  2. ✅ Financing Pipeline — Loan origination commissions (5-12%)
  3. ✅ Mechanic Labor — Service network commissions (20-30%)
  4. ✅ Parts Flow — Parts supply margins (10-20%)
  5. ✅ Fleet Assets — Leasing revenue ($700-$900/vehicle/month)
  6. ✅ Driver Data — Telematics, behavior analytics, credit scoring
  7. ✅ Energy (EV + Solar) — Charging infrastructure + fleet electrification

How the Platform Works (Transaction Flow)

Step 1: Vehicle Listed on Marketplace

  • Dealer or private seller uploads vehicle
  • Platform requires: Photos, specs, inspection report ($50 fee)
  • AI-powered pricing suggestion (based on transaction data)
  • Featured listing option ($50-$200 for premium placement)

Step 2: Buyer Discovers Vehicle

  • Search filters: Make, model, price, location, features
  • Personalized recommendations (ML-based)
  • Saved searches + email alerts
  • In-platform messaging with seller

Step 3: Financing + Insurance (THE MONEY LAYER)

This is where you capture massive commissions:

  • Financing Calculator: Buyer enters income, down payment
  • Lender Matching: Platform routes to 3-5 bank/MFI partners
  • Parallel Applications: Submit to multiple lenders simultaneously
  • Approval + Commission: 5-12% of loan value = $500-$1,200 per $10k car
  • Insurance Quote Engine: Multi-provider comparison
  • Broker Commission: 15-25% annually on premium

Step 4: Transaction Completion

  • Digital contract signing (e-signature)
  • Payment processing (escrow optional)

Step 5: Post-Sale Services (LIFETIME VALUE)

The transaction doesn't end at sale. It STARTS there.

  • First Maintenance: Route to certified mechanic (20-30% commission)
  • Parts Purchases: Route through your supply chain (10-20% margin)
  • Insurance Renewal: Annual recurring commission (15-25%)
  • Eventual Resale: Customer trades in on platform → another commission cycle

Platform Network Effects:

  • More buyers → More sellers join
  • More sellers → More vehicle selection → More buyers
  • More transactions → Better data → Better predictions → More trust
  • More mechanics → Faster service → Better customer experience
  • More customers → More parts volume → Better supplier pricing

This is a compounding system. Each layer feeds the others.

Data Licensing: The Hidden Billion-Dollar Asset

Every transaction generates data. After 1000+ transactions, you have the ONLY comprehensive automotive dataset in your market.

What Data You Collect

  1. Resale Values: What cars actually sell for (not asking prices)
  2. Default Rates: Which borrowers default on loans (by demographics, vehicle type)
  3. Maintenance Costs: True cost of ownership by make/model/year
  4. Driver Behavior: Telematics from fleet vehicles (accidents, fuel efficiency)
  5. Insurance Claims: Which vehicles are high risk
  6. Regional Demand: Which cars sell faster in which cities
